07 — B.Tech Thesis Research

Air Pollution Hotspot Mapping

Spatial analysis of particulate-matter and gaseous-pollutant hotspots — B.Tech thesis under Dr. Sharad Gokhale at IIT Guwahati.

IIT-G
B.Tech thesis, 2022–2023
PM2.5
Primary pollutant studied
GIS
Spatial analysis method
Folium
Interactive map output

Thesis overview

Identification and spatial characterisation of air-quality hotspots in a medium-sized Indian city. The study analysed PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, and CO readings from continuous monitoring stations alongside meteorological data (wind speed/direction, temperature, humidity) to map pollutant dispersion patterns.

Methodology

Sensor data was cleaned, normalised, and geo-referenced. Kriging interpolation (via ArcGIS / Python) was used to generate spatial concentration surfaces. Hotspots were identified using Kernel Density Estimation and validated against traffic flow and industrial source data. Correlation analysised the dominant emission sources for each pollutant.

Key findings

Peak PM2.5 concentrations were 4.2× the WHO annual guideline at identified hotspots. Traffic-related emissions dominated for NO₂ (62% correlation with vehicle count), while PM2.5 hotspots overlapped significantly with construction and biomass-burning zones. Wind-direction analysis showed clear seasonal transport patterns from industrial corridors.

Visualisation tools

Folium interactive maps display hotspot clusters with drill-down time-series charts per monitoring station. Seaborn heatmaps show diurnal and seasonal pollution patterns. All outputs are reproducible via the Jupyter notebook pipeline included in the repository.
